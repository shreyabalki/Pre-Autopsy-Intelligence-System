Problem & Solution NeuroForensic AI
The Problem

No fast unified tool for field investigators

Investigators in low-resource settings must wait days for specialist review — across body evidence, brain scans, and toxicology separately. Cases go cold.

  • Geographic barrier — specialists concentrated in cities, unreachable for rural investigators
  • Time barrier — days of wait time; evidence degrades, suspects move
  • Multi-modal gap — radiology, toxicology, and trauma analysis exist in separate siloed tools
  • Cost barrier — sending physical samples or images to labs is slow and expensive
The Solution

NeuroForensic AI — any investigator, anywhere

Upload evidence → AI analyzes → structured report in under 60 seconds. No specialist on-site required. Runs from a laptop.

Ethics, Guardrails & Roadmap NeuroForensic AI
Ethical Framework

Safety Built Into Every Layer

Every design decision prioritises investigator safety and prevents over-reliance on AI output.

✓ What it IS
AI-powered forensic screening tool
Decision-support for investigators
INTERPOL DVI protocol-aligned language
Mandatory expert-review disclaimer on every report
Only public anonymised datasets used
✗ What it is NOT
Not a clinical diagnostic tool
Not a cause-of-death determiner
Not a legal evidence generator
Not trained on private patient data
Not a replacement for pathologists
Prompt guardrails: AI is explicitly prohibited from naming diseases, stating cause of death, inventing demographics, or claiming certainty. All findings use hedged language — "consistent with", "suggests", "warrants review".
